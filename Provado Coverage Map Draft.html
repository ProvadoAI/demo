Coverage states — how to read the colours. Every finding is detect → trace → price; the trace (cross-stack causal trace to the operational root) and the price are always Provado's. A box's colour describes only who detects, and whether we can depend on that detector existing in the ICP.
 • Own — Provado builds the detection. Chosen whenever we can't depend on the merchant having a detecting tool: either no tool exists, or — more often — a tool exists but ICP adoption is thin or unknown, so we build our own (often lightweight) detector rather than take the dependency.
 • Consume & trace — a detecting tool is ICP-near-universal (APM, Google Merchant Center, GA4, Algolia where present), so depending on its signal is safe and rebuilding it would be waste plus a credibility risk in front of a technical buyer. We ingest its detection as the symptom; we own the trace + price.
 • Anchor (SoR) — read-only system-of-record truth Provado reads as ground truth (order/revenue record, product master, stock record). Not a detection; never edited.
 • 3P covers — a third party owns the capability itself and there is no operational cause for us to trace (ranking/synonyms, recommendation models, full session-replay analytics). We consume output where useful, build nothing. Where such a product is deep but adoption is thin (Profound, Noibu), Provado still carries a lightweight tripwire — and that tripwire is Own, not 3P-covers.
 • Out — not our problem: business judgment (pricing/merchandising strategy, facet design, CRO), legal (consent compliance), off-thesis (channel attribution).  • Validate scope — in scope in principle, pending discovery to confirm whether and how Provado builds it.
Dual chips (e.g. Consume & trace Own*) are not indecision: they mark an adoption branch — Consume where the near-universal tool is present, Own where it isn't — with the asterisk pointing at the discovery question that resolves it. At Layer 3 (revenue surfaces) the chips are problems the merchant feels, shown in neutral grey — they carry no coverage colour; the states above apply only at Layers 0–2. Inside each surface a disposition says what Provado does with each problem. ✓ Diagnose — Provado produces the diagnosis (cross-stack trace → price → recommend the fix); the underlying symptom is either Provado-detected or read from a tool the merchant already runs (Consume & trace) — the row and the trace below say which. The trace itself is always Provado's; no third party does it. Provado produces this diagnosis when the cause is an operational fault in the layers it monitors and ingests. Many of these symptoms can also have causes outside that purview — demand, price, product desirability, merchandising and UX judgment, competitive shifts — and when the cause is outside what Provado watches, the honest and complete finding is "no store-side cause found." Provado rules its own layers in or out; it does not explain the symptom away. ⊘ Out — felt here, but no operational cause Provado can trace; the row names who does own it. ⏸ Validate scope — in scope in principle, pending discovery to confirm whether and how Provado builds it. This is a working model, not a settled spec: as diagnosis goes deeper on real merchants a box’s status may move, so treat every state as current-best, not final.
Naming key: Layer = depth band · Domain = titled box · Capability = cluster (in Layer 3 these are channels) · Component = the chip. The orchestrating engine connects agent findings up to the surfaces and prices them.
Agent tags are a hypothesis, not a commitment. Tags below organize agents by cause-domain (the right principle per the coverage-states decision), but the final agent decomposition is an architecture decision owned by engineering — in particular the COMMERCE tag spans search, pricing, checkout, and content, and may well split into multiple agents. Do not build against these tags until the architecture session settles them.

CROSS-LAYEROrchestrating engine
Knowledge engine + attribution engine. No single agent sees across layers. The engine takes each agent's cause-domain findings (below), traces the fan-out up to the surfaces (above), correlates symptom ↔ cause by time-window and failure pattern, and prices the confirmed fault. What makes it lightweight: it verifies machine-checkable preconditions against requirements — it never queries the AI engines to read actual answers. That is what keeps it cheap to build and off the measurement vendors' moat.

Why consume, not build — the same discipline as APM and Merchant Center. The commerce platforms now ship native AI-readiness: Shopify scores catalog completeness for AI and one-click-publishes products to ChatGPT & Gemini; Adobe's Catalog Agent diffs the catalog against the agent-visible page and flags missing attributes; commercetools exposes the catalog to agents through its Commerce MCP (and calls AI discovery commoditizing). When a detecting capability becomes near-universal in the category, Provado consumes its signal rather than rebuilding it — exactly as it does with bundled APM (latency) and Google Merchant Center (disapprovals). But a capability existing is not every mid-market merchant having it configured — so the state is consume where present, thin tripwire where it is not, never Out. The platform stops at the eligibility checkbox; the upstream cause, the source-of-record truth, the cross-surface blast radius, and the price stay always Provado's.

The honesty boundary — necessary, not sufficient. A product can pass every eligibility check and still not be recommended, because AI ranking also depends on relevance, brand authority, and competitive content — none machine-checkable, none Provado's job. So Provado verifies nothing operational is suppressing visibility, and names the cause when something is; it cannot guarantee a product will be visible. Same discipline as "not us, among what we watch." It is also why Profound is genuinely complementary: it sees the outcome, including the competitive / share-of-voice dimension Provado deliberately leaves Out — it localizes no operational cause, so it fails the detect→trace→price test.

PRODUCT & CATALOG (PIM) — expanded

Provado does two jobs here. First it validates — checks the product data against a known standard: are the required fields present, do the copies across systems agree, does the product clear each channel's bar to appear. Then, when a check fails, it diagnoses — traces the failure to the operational event that caused it and prices the impact. It does not write content, decide which system is authoritative, or design the catalog structure — those are creation and judgment, not checking. The line: if an activity is a fact checked against a requirement, it's Provado's; if it needs creating or judging, it's someone else's.
